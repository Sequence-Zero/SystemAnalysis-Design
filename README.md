# SystemAnalysis-Design
Final Reflections on System Design projects
The purpose of this project is to document and design a system that supports the DriverPass client in delivering online driving education, practice exams, and on the road training services. The client is a company with a mission focused on helping students better prepare for the DMV driving tests by combining online learning with in person instruction. They would like their system to be able to register accounts, purchase training packages, and schedule driving lessons. Users should also be able to take online practice exams and track their progress throughout their learning. The system also needed to be able to support internal staff roles which includes administrators, IT, and office staff to manage users, appointments, and the data of the system. 
One area I performed particularly well in was translating client needs from the interview transcript into clearly structured functional and nonfunctional requirements. I ensured that performance expectations, security safeguards, platform constraints, and scalability were explicitly defined, which strengthened the overall feasibility of the design. If I were to revise one component, I would further refine the system design diagrams to better illustrate data flow between scheduling, payment processing, and exam tracking components.
When interpreting user needs, I focused on identifying pain points, such as failed DMV attempts and scheduling inefficiencies, aligning each system feature with a measurable objective. Considering user needs is critical because a technically sound system that fails to address real world workflow challenges will not deliver value.
My approach to designing software begins with understanding the problem domain and stakeholders before introducing technical solutions. I prioritize requirements gathering, role identification, and workflow mapping prior to system modeling. Moving forward, I would continue using structured requirement analysis, UML modeling, iterative refinement, and traceability between requirements and design artifacts.
